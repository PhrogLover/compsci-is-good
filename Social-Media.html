Social Media

Social media is becoming an increasingly important part of our lives, particularly during COVID, allowing us to create and maintain relationships with many more people than we would be able to interact with in our daily lives. From finding you next job opportunity to your future spouse technology has boosted the realms of human relationships far beyond what could have previously been imagined.

From a psychological perspective it would make sense that theoretically social media would make people feel more connected to the world and others resulting in an overall decrease in loneliness. However, a 2018 study evaluating exactly this found that not all social media were equal in this idea. Since social media only has a positive effect when the user perceives social media as intimate and a good way of connecting to people whereas if the user was already lonely and they view social media as not particularly intimate their social media use becomes more alienating than connecting. However social media has also increased people’s access to therapy and online support groups which also benefit these people who perhaps do not have the relevant or volume of resources necessary to help them.

Logos of key social media

There are many different ways that social media has changed people’s job prospects. As social media itself has become its own job market with thousands of ‘influencers’ over various platforms, e.g. Instagram, YouTube, whose skills and backgrounds vary dramatically tapping into a brand-new style of marketing viable for almost any product. Whereas there is also professional focused social media which boosts peoples’ international career opportunities and professional networks. LinkedIn is incredibly popular with 660 million plus users as of 2020, despite the fact LinkedIn is just a social media site it has created its own sector by having a more formal and professional image which differentiates itself from others and makes it the standard for professional social media.

Due to COVID the world has been forced to isolate and work out ways to connect without being physically in the same room but it has also simultaneously proved that business and socials can function and continue mostly (if not solely) online which could potentially open a whole world of possibilities in terms of working from home to a groups ability to function without a physical meeting place.
